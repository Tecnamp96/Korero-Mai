SPEAK TO ME · SPEAK TOWARD ME

Kōrero Mai

A transitional way for verified New Zealand citizens to signal how they want their MPs to vote — before parliament votes, not after.

Built for the window between Integrity New Zealand being sworn in and the Citizens' Senate being established. It produces a signal, not a mandate — and it is designed, from day one, to close.

Advisory, not binding Published before the vote Built to close
Live prototype — verify, tap a bill, read the brief to unlock signalling, then see the public result.
NGĀ MĀTĀPONO · WHAT MAKES IT NOT A POLL

Transparency is the architecture, not a feature

The reading gate
Te tatau pānui

Signalling stays locked until you've read the independent brief. It proves the brief was read, not bypassed.

Read vs tap, public
Kounga whaiwāhi

Results show what share read the brief first. 58% oppose from readers is a different signal to 58% from tappers.

Mandatory explanation
Te whakamārama

If an MP votes against the signal, they file a public explanation within 24 hours — surfaced right in the app.

The sunset clause
Te rā tō

A visible progress tracker toward the Citizens' Senate. When it convenes, the app closes and the archive goes public.

NGĀ MATA E RIMA · THE FIVE SCREENS

The whole flow, end to end

Each screen below is live — tap through any of them. Verification starts the flow; the Results screen shows a real divergence case, with the MP's mandatory explanation.

01Whakaū · Verify
One-time onboarding. RealMe identity matched to the electoral roll — one citizen, one signal, then anonymised.

Independent plain-language brief with the reading gate. Signalling unlocks only once read.

Three bilingual options — Tautoko, Whakahē, Hiahia mōhio anō. Anonymous, published before the vote.
05Ngā hua · Results
Signal split, engagement quality, the parliamentary vote, divergence explanation, and the sunset tracker.
HE AHA TĒNEI · WHAT IT IS
✓A verified, electoral-roll-matched signal from any NZ citizen
✓Published before INZ casts its parliamentary vote
✓A transitional bridge to the Citizens' Senate
EHARA I TĒNEI · WHAT IT IS NOT
✕Not a binding mandate — it informs and pressures, it doesn't direct
✕Not an INZ membership tool — open to all verified citizens
✕Not permanent — it closes when the Senate is established
